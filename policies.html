Operational Manual & Policies

1.00 Introduction

All personnel are expected to uphold the values of Fire and Emergency NZ, prioritizing life safety, incident stabilization, and property conservation in all actions.

1.01 Staff Conduct and Operational Interaction

Members must maintain professional conduct when interacting with other agencies. Chain of command must be respected at all times during active incidents.

1.02 Radio Guidelines

Practice Radio Silence during critical incident phases. Use "Clear Speech" instead of complex codes to ensure all responding units understand instructions.

1.03 Callsign Guideline

Callsigns are assigned based on the appliance and district. Personnel must use their vehicle callsign (e.g., CHRI217) rather than personal names on all frequencies.

1.04 Radio Codes

Standardized status codes (K1, K2, K3, K4) must be used to update Dispatch on arrival, readiness, and scene status without clogging airtime.

1.05 Radio Events

In the event of a "Priority 1" or "Emergency Traffic" call, all other units must cease transmission immediately until the emergency is cleared.

1.06 Equipment Basics/Usages

Specialized equipment such as the Jaws of Life, thermal cameras, and gas detectors may only be operated by personnel who have completed the relevant training modules.

1.07 Uniform Guidelines

High-visibility vests are mandatory for all roadway incidents. Full structural PPE including flash hoods is required for any interior fire operations.

1.08 Vehicle Guidelines

CHRI217 must be fully crewed with 4 personnel before secondary trucks are utilized. Drivers must perform a 360-degree walk-around before moving any appliance.

1.09 Airdesk Guidelines

Coordination with aerial assets requires a designated ground spotter. Units must maintain a 50m exclusion zone from landing sites for helicopter operations.

1.10 Operational Support Unit (OSU) Deployment

OSU units provide lighting, hydration, and rehab. They should be staged at a "Cold Zone" location and marked clearly for all personnel to access.

1.11 MEDFR Deployment

Medical First Response units are dispatched for high-acuity medical calls. Personnel must provide a handover to Ambulance staff upon their arrival.
