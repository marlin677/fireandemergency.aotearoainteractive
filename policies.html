NATIONAL OPERATIONAL MANUAL

Chapter 1: Communications & Conduct
1.01Core Values

Prioritise life safety, incident stabilisation, and property conservation.

1.02Chain of Command

Incident Commander (IC) holds ultimate authority. Orders must be executed immediately.

1.03Radio Silence

Mandatory during critical phases or Mayday calls. No non-emergency traffic.

1.04K1 - Proceeding

Appliance is mobile and en route to the incident location.

1.05K2 - On Scene

Arrival at incident. Provide a SITREP within 60 seconds.

1.06K3 - Controlled

Incident is under control but unit is remaining for further support.

1.07K4 - Mobile Available

Appliance has left the scene and is ready for the next dispatch.

1.08K5 - In Quarters

Appliance is back at the station and available for response.

1.09K9 - Off Duty

Unit is unavailable due to mechanical failure or end of shift.

1.10Clear Speech

Use plain English at all times. Do not use complex 10-codes.

Chapter 2: Fireground Operations
2.00Appliance Staffing

Primary pumps must have a crew of 4 (Officer, Driver, 2 FFs) for structural fires.

2.01Emergency Evacuation

Three long siren blasts signal immediate evacuation of all personnel from a structure.

2.02BA Entry Control

No entry into smoke without a designated Entry Control Officer (ECO).

2.03LACES Protocol

Lookouts, Awareness, Communications, Escape routes, and Safety zones for wildfires.

2.04Response Driving

Blue lights/sirens do not grant total immunity. Stop at all red lights.

2.05Roadway Fend-Off

Park appliances at 45 degrees to protect crews from traffic.
